Source of Funds & Income

  • Boniface Kinyori
  • Nov 10, 2022

A business has various ways of mobilizing financial resources required to finance plans. These ways are broadly classified as sources of funds and sources of income. For sources of income, a business engages in income generating activities which generate various income for the business. For sources of funds, on the other hand, a business mobilizes funding from various providers of funding who do so of an associated cost.

Sources of funds

Its classified under various categories i.e.
long-term sources of funding and short-term sources of funding internal sources and external sources of funds.

Long-term and short-term sources

Classification of funding based on the maturity period of the funds. The period within which the funding is repayable. Due consideration should be given to the repayment period to ensure that various sources of funding are directed to financing various ../assets / initiatives which will pay back within the same period.

Long-term sources of funds

When the funding is mobilized is repayable after a long period of time usually exceeding 5 years it might be consider as a long-term source of fund. Long term sources of fund have the characteristics that they are used to raise large amount of money and the financing of capital items/ expenditures (items that will last in the business for a long period of time usually beyond 1 year i.e. land, building, furniture and equipment) . Long term sources of funds: share capital, long-term loans, lease financing, venture capital, mortgages, retained profits.

a. Shared capital: Refers to funding raised through the sale of a company’s shares/ ownership. There are two 2 types of shares that a company can sell which include ordinary shares and preference shares.

Ordinary shares/ equity: shares that are bought by ordinary shareholder of the company. These are shareholders who are actual owners on the business who therefor share in the profits and losses of the business and are involved in the decision of the company therefor they have control over the management of the company.
Preference shares: are preferred shares in the sense that they share in the profits whether the business makes profits or loses. This is though predetermined percentage which is fixed whether the business makes profit or losses. Preference shareholders are not involved in the decision making of the organization and also not involved in the voting for various decision of the company. In sharing of profits preference stakeholder are considered first before the ordinary shareholders. The associated cost with the share capital is dividends (share of profits) shares out with new shareholders.

Advantages of capital shares
Finances raised does not need to be paid back.
Large amounts of finance can be raised.

Disadvantages of capital shares.
Shareholders need to be paid dividends each year.
Shareholders become part owners of the business.

  

b. Venture Capital

A form of financing that is provided to small, early stage emerging business (startups) that are deemed to have high potential. The providers of this finances maybe venture capital firms, individuals. They are often referred to as Venture capitalists. They take up the risk of financing the risky startups in the hope that they will become in the future. Venture capital is a key feature in innovative business today that is business that are adopting the now technologies. The associated cost as a source of fund is liquidation of ownership which requires that profits are shares out within the venture capitalists once they take ownership stake and funding your business.

Advantages of venture capital.
Available for more risky investments.

Disadvantages of venture capital
A large return maybe required due to the high-risk nature of the investments. Venture capitalist may want shares of the business meaning some control may be lost.

  c. Long term loans (interest → associated cos)

Loans provided by financial institutions such as banks. They are loans which are payable in less than 5 years. Before a bank gives the loan, they must prove that the business has technical, economical, commercial, financial and managerial viability. This prove of viability is given by a business plan. To raise such loans a bank requires security in form of ../assets to guarantee the bank of payment should the business fair to pay the loan. The ../assets maybe land (title deeds), vehicles (logbook), share capital (share certification). The associated cost with loans is the interests with which are amount borrowed will be ventured.

d. Retained earnings/ profits

From the profits the company makes it may decide not to share out all of it with the stakeholder as dividends. This part of profit that the stakeholder decides to put back into the business is referred to as retained earnings. Retained earnings belong to the ordinary shareholders. They therefore add to the ordinary share capital/ equity of each shareholder. It is an appropriate source of financing that is easy to raise, cheap and doesn’t dilute the ownership of the present owners. The associated cost with returned earnings is there for increased future dividends to shareholders.

Advantages of retained profits
Doesn’t need to be paid
full control of business is maintained

Disadvantages of retained profits
For profits to build up to use this way it can take a long time and business opportunities missed.
Personal savings may be lost if the business is unsuccessful.

e. Lease financing (rent associated cost)

This is a contract where one party agrees to rent property to another party for an agreed period of time. The use of ../assets and also guarantee the lessor (landlord) regular payment for the period agreed. Both the leaser and the lessee consequences are they break the contract. The associated coat is therefore the regular payment that the lessee makes.

Advantages of lease financing
large amounts of money are required up front to lease machinery.
The leasing company are responsible for repairs and maintenance.

Disadvantages of leasing financing
over time it can be an expensive way to obtain ../assets.
../assets are not owned by the business.

f. Mortgages

This is a source of fund (loan) from a bank that is advanced to especially private individuals for purposes of financing the acquisition of residential property.

Advantages of mortgages
mortgage is given for a long period of time.
Large amounts of finance can be raised quickly.

Disadvantages of mortgage
Interest is charged on the loan.
Property can be lost to the mortgage lender if payment is missed.

Associated cost
Retained profit – Dividends
share capital – Dividends
long- term loans – interest
leasing finance-- rent
mortgage – rent
venture capital – liquidation of ownership

Short term sources of funds

Funds that are refundable after to a short period of time usually within 2year therefore making available for a shorter period of time.

Purposes of short-term sources

a) Facilitates smooth running of a business operations by meeting day by day financial requirements.
b) Enables the firm to hold stock of raw materials and financial products.
c) With the availability of short-term finances goods can be sold on credit, correction of money from debtors takes time during this time production still continues therefore money is needed to finance various operations. Short-term loans provide this money.
d) It’s essential when it is necessary to increase the volume of production as a short-term notice or when such market opportunities arise.
e) They allow for flow of cash during the operating cycle: refers to the time gap between commencement of production and realization of sales.

a. Trade credit (creditors)

Refers to credit granted to us by manufacturers, traders/ supplies of the raw materials, finished goods or companies that we use. Usually move business enterprises acquire their supplies onto a 30 or so days credit period, this means that goods are delivered but payment are not made until credit period is over. Essentially this credit isn’t available in cash but it facilitates purchase without making payments.

b. Bank Credits

Advanced to a business by a commercial bank. Its granted in the following ways: -

short-term loans: loan from a bank repayable after a specified short period such as loan is granted against a security/ collateral of ../assets (logbook, title deeds) the borrower has to pay back the principal amount borrowed plus interest.
Bank overdraft: when a bank allows its accounts holders to withdraw money in excess of the balance, such is known as bank overdraft. The money allowed depends on the credit withdraw of the borrower.
Discounting a bill of exchange: Applies in international trade where one party commits to pay another a determined amount of money on a future date. An individual holding this bill of exchange can present it to their bank where the bank takes custody and ownership of the bill and in return, they discount the amount of the bill.

c. Customer advances

Some business may request customers to pay in advance e.g. when the value of the customer order is large/ costly also when such goods are urgently needed. Through such advance payment from customers for goods to be delivered in a future date, a film can meet its short terms financial requirements.

d. Installment credit

This is a popular source of finance that applies when a business is acquiring ../assets such as land, motor vehicles etc. It requires that the business pay a deposit and the balance is paid in future installments (hire purchase system). This balance is therefore a credit that is advanced to our business allowing it to acquire the ../assets as well as redirect money to other short-term uses.

e. Promissory note.

It’s similar to bill of exchange. Applies in international trade whereby one party promises to pay another in writing a defined sum of money in future. With such promissory note a business can provide it as security that allows it to get financing from bank.

f. Letter of credit.

A letter from a bank guaranteeing that a buyer’s payment to a seller will be received on time and fore the correct amount. In the event that the buyer is unable to make than payment the bank will be required to cover the pending amount. Also applies in international trade.

Advantages of short-term sources of finance.
A quick payout to get cash you need when you need it.
A lending opportunity still available to borrowers with had credit.
Flexibility in payment options and structure.
uses time to acquire interest.

Disadvantages of short-term sources of finance.
High interest rates.
Debt cycle, short-term sources are best for unexpected emerging situations.
Short terms sources of finances mature more frequently.
Higher installments payments, as they come with more flexible repayment requirements.
Early payment penalties.
Limited finance provided compared to long-term loans.

Internal and External Sources.

Internal sources: - this refers to money that comes from sources within the business. There are several internal methods a business can use including owners; -

Retained profits – if a business makes a profit, it has the option to leave some or all the money in the business. It can then be re-invested to allow the business to expand.

External sources – cash flows generated from outside sources if the organization whether from private means or from the financial market e.g. bank loans, bank overdraft, venture capital. External sources can be sub-divided into two branches namely equity financing & debt financing

Debt financing: - source of finance where fixed payment has to be made to the lender. In debt financing include: bank loans, corporate loans, trade credit, leasing, commercial paper, debentures.
Equity financing: - major source of financing for most of the companies which indicate the share in the ownership of the firm and the interest of the shareholders. The firm’s raw capital by selling its shares to the investors, it includes: ordinary share, preference shares.

Sources of incom

These are revenue generated from various activities that an organization engages in. In tourism and hospitality industry they include: sales, commission, grants, sponsorship, sub-renting.

a. Sales
This is the main activity for most organizations in the hospitality industry. It is the activity of selling the business products and services e.g. food and beverage, accommodation, conference and banqueting.

b. Commission
Can be generated in 2 ways:

1. Commission has as a way of rewarding the sales employees is an income generating activity in the sense that it pushes employees to make more sales thereby generating more sales income.
2. A hotel also earns commission by acting as the agent for their guests. Through the agency relationship they do services on behalf of their guests. E.g. acquiring booking tickets, booking for safaris etc. they are paid in commission for their undertaking.

c. Grants
An amount of money given by the government to an organization for a special purpose. For any organization to qualify for a grant they must meet certain conditions set by the giver of the grant.

d. Sponsorship
This is a source of income for an organization which allow other organization (sponsor) to be associated with a specific event. The sponsor aims to use the event to market themselves or improve their image. In return they will contribute money partly or fully to fund the event.

e. Sub-letting
Rent income generated from spare/ empty spaces that the organization rents out to interested individuals.
