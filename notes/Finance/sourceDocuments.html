Sources Documents

  • Boniface Kingori
  • Nov 10, 2022

These are documents that show evidence of transactions. A transaction is anything that happens in a business that has a financial implication when a transaction happens, they are therefore captured by the source’s documents. These documents are the source of the information that is eventually summarized in their financial statements. Source documents include: receipts, sale invoices, purchase invoices, credit note, cheque.

1. Receipts
A receipt may be given by a firm or received by a firm when the firm makes a cash sale and a cash purchase respectively;
Receipts contains:

name and address of the issuing firm, date of the sale.
Amount received and the description of the goods. Receipt number.

2. Sales invoice
Prepared by our firm or given to a debtor/ customer when our firm makes a credit sale to them.
It contains:

name and address of firm.
Name and address of the debtor customer.
Date of the sale.
Description of the goods sold.
Amount due to their terms and conditions of the sale.

3. Purchase invoice
Prepared by our creditor (a person from we buy goods on credit) and given to our firm when our firm makes a credit purchase.
Contains:

name and address of the creditor seller.
Name and address of our firm, date of the purchase.
Amount due.
Invoice number.
Description of goods bought.
Terms and conditions of the sale.

4. Debit Note
Prepared by our creditors/ supplier and issued to our firm when we return some goods to the creditor (return outward). Its purpose is to inform us that the amount due to the creditor has been reduced/ cancelled.
Contains:

name and address of our firm.
Amount of debit.
Name and address of the creditor.
Debit note number.
Reason for return outwards.

5. Credit note
For goods already sold by our fir they may be returned to us due to various reasons such are (return inward). Prepared by our firm and issued by the debtor customer when the debtor returns some goods back to our firm. Serves to inform the debtor/ customer that the amount owning from has been reduced/ cancelled.
Contains:

name and address of the firm.
Name and address of the debtor.
Amount of credit.
Credit number
Reason and return inwards.

6. Petty cash voucher
Prepared by a cashier to show authorization of payment of small value in the firm which requires a cash payment e.g. fuel, office snack, cleaning material etc.
Contains:

date of payment.
Amount paid.
Reason for bringing it.
Person receiving.
Authorized signatures.

Person receiving the money must then return a document showing how the money was utilized.

7. Cheque
When a firm opens a current account with the bank a cheque book containing cheques is issued. The cheque allows the firm to make payments against the accounts with the bank, when a firm issues a cheque to its creditors for payment it authorizes the bank to honor payment.
Contains:

the name and account number of the firm.
Date of the cheque.
Name of payee.
Amount payable in words and figures.
Cheque number.
Authorized signatures.

8. Other correspondences
Includes information received within or outside the firm that has a financial impression in the business e.g. letters from the firm’s lawyers about owing legal fees, higher purchase agreements that relates to non-current ../assets.

Also called books of prime entry, they summarize the source documents. To patch and prey source documents there is a corresponding book of original entry. This is as illustrated below.

5. Cashbook
It records all the receipts (cash & received cheques) from customers and all the payment (to creditors, suppliers and other expenses) for a particular financial period. The cashbook will therefore show the cash at the bank and in hand at any point in time. There are two types of cashbooks: two-column cashbook & three column cashbooks.

6. Petty cashbook
A record that summarizes all the petty cash vouchers raised and kept by the cashier, therefore summarizes all the expenses paid for by the cashier with these expenses being listed and classified in the petty cashbook under relevant heading or expenses.
