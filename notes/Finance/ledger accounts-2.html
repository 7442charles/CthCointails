Ledger Accounts

  • Boniface Kingori
  • Nov 10, 2022

Accounting for other specific items.

Accounting for drawings.
When the owner of a business takes anything from the business e.g. cash, stock, personal expenses paid by the business or anything of the business for his personal need this is a drawing.
Drawings are related to capital by sense that capital decreases.
Decrease in capital will be debited. The accounting treatment for drawing therefore is :

a. Increase in drawings are debited.
b. The account of the item drawn is credited.

Accounting for returns inwards and return outwards.
Returns inwards and return outwards affect stock by increasing and decreasing stock respectively.
Return inwards: goods that have been returned to business by our customers due to various reasons.
The accounting treatment for return inwards is that:

a. Increase in return inwards are debited.
b. Credit the specific debtors account if the returned goods had been sold on credit or credit cash or bank account for cash refunded if goods had been sold by cash.

Return outwards: goods that we return to our suppliers or creditors.
The accounting treatment for return outwards is that:

a. Credit the returns outwards accounts.
b. Debit the specific creditor liability account for goods that had been bought on credit (decrease in liability) or debit the cash/ bank account for cash refunded to us (business) if the goods has been bought by cash.

Accounting for sales and purchases.
The sales of goods that were bought by a business with the intention of reselling.
Sales are divided into cash sales and credit sales. When a cash sale is made, the accounting entries recorded are:

a. Credit sales account.
b. Debit the cash/ bank account.

When a credit sale is made to accounting entries are:

a. Credit the sales account.
b. Debit the specific debtors account.

Purchases
The buying of goods that are made for resell. Purchases are also done on credit or by cash.

When we buy goods by cash the following are made:

a. Debit the purchase account.
b. Credit the specific creditor account.

Accounting for discount
A business has two types if discount:

i. Trade/ quantity discount.
ii. Cash discount.

A quantity discount is given discriminatively to customers for buying more over a given quantity.
It is not consistent and therefore not recognized in accounting, Cash discount in the other hand it is the discount that is recognized in accounting.
It is a discount that is expressed in the terms and condition of a sale with the intention of encouraging the customer to pay some i.e., a higher discount is given to customer who pay by cash and this rate of discount decreases as the credit period of the customer increases. Discount in a business maybe discount allowed to our customer when selling or discount received from our suppliers when making a purchase.

Entries for discount allowed
The accounting entries for discount allowed.

i. Debit the discount allowed because discount allowed is an expense.
ii. Credit the specific debtor account.

Entries for discount received.

i. Credit the discount received account.
ii. Debit the specific creditor account.

Balancing of ledger accounts.

Leger accounts are deemed incomplete if they are not balanced off.
Balancing of ledger accounts entails a comparison of the debit and credit side if an account, the determination of the difference between the two sides and use of this difference to balance off.
This balancing off entails the following steps:

i. Find the total of both sides of an account.
ii. Compare and find the difference between the two sides.
iii. Using the difference between the two sides add it to the side with the smallest figure to achieve balance of the two sides and double underline.

This balancing figure is known as balanced carried down abbreviated as bal c/d.
Indicate the following figure on the opposite side of the balance claimed down as a balance brought down abbreviated as bal b/d.
This balance brought down is the figure that serves as the opening balance for their account in the next financial period, Also it is the figure that is used in the preparation of final statement.
It should be observed that:

a. The balance brought down of ../assets account is usually a debit balance.
b. The balance brought down if liability account is usually a credit balance.
c. The balance brought down of the capital account is usually a credit balance.
d. The balance brought down of income accounts is usually a credit balance.
