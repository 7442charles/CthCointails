Challenges of Customer Service

  • Madam Lucy
  • Nov 10, 2022
  • Chapter 2

Customer service is such a valuable concept that it seems it would be simple to provide it. Unfortunately, this is not necessarily the case.
Customer service providers should asses their strengths and weakness to understand how better to server or have served their customers.

Barriers to Excellent Customer Service

Numerous obstacles stand in the way of the delivery of excellent customer service.
Some barriers to excellent customer service are within the control of the customer service provider.
These challenges can be overcome through diligent effort, allowing the customer service provider to do the best possible job.
Some of the most common barriers to excellent customer service are:-

1. Laziness
2. Poor communication skills
3. Poor time management
4. Attitude
5. Moodiness
6. Lack of adequate training
7. Inability to handle stress
8. Insufficient authority
9. Serving customers on autopilot
10. Inadequate staffing

Understanding Perception and Expectation.

perception is the way that we see something based on our experience.
It is important for the customer service provider to anticipate customer resistance based on the customers’ prior interactions and always to work at providing the customers with excellent service so that their most current perception is a positive one.
There are various ways to make sure the customer leaves with a positive perception on your company or the organization.
This ways include and are not limited to:

1. Respect their time, circumstances, and priorities.
2. Appreciate them for doing business with your company.
3. Ask them if there is something else you can do for them.
4. Periodically ask them how they are doing.

Expectations are our personal vision of the result that will come from our experience.
Expectation may be positive or negative. Expectations are usually based, at least partially, on our perceptions.
Customer service providers must recognize that customers have different levels of expectations.

1. Primary expectations are the customers’ most basic requirements of an interaction. e.g When dining at a restaurant, our primary expectations are to satisfy our hunger
2. Secondary expectations are expectations based on our previous expe- riences that are enhancements to our primary expectations. When dining at a restaurant, our secondary expectations are to have good service; to be treated with courtesy; and to receive good, tasty food.

Reputation Management

Reputation management is the process of identifying how a company is perceived and establishing an action plan to correct, maintain, or enhance the company’s reputation.
A company seeking to begin to manage its reputation must survey its customers and the community at large to assess what its reputation really is.
Characteristics of a company with a good reputation:

1. Is trustworthy
2. Demonstrates excellence in its management at all levels
3. Is law abiding
4. Is responsive
5. Follows through on promises and commitments
6. Is ethically responsible
7. Is a positive public citizen
8. Has accurate and strong financials
9. Communicates effectively
10. Shows ongoing positive social responsibility

Technique of exceeding Customer Expectations

1. Become familiar with your customers.
2. Ask your customers what their expectations are.
3. Tell your customers what they can expect.
4. Live up to their expectations.
5. Maintain consistency.
6. Communicate with customers using the method they want to use.

Key to Credibility

Credibility is made up of the combination of our current knowledge, reputation, and professionalism.
Credibility encourages trust.
Our personal credibility can be the one characteristic that determines our success as people and as customer service providers.
Ways to cultivate Credibility:

1. Practice consistency.
2. Keep your word.
3. Develop your expertise.
4. Become a teammate with your coworkers.
5. Show your dedication to customers.
6. Treat all of your customers and coworkers with the same high level of respect.
7. Apologize if you are wrong.

Ethics in Customer Service

Ethics are a set of principles that govern the conduct of an individual or group.
These principles are useful in decision making in that individuals must face the consequences of their decisions—good or bad.
Questions to ask yourself when making an ethical decision:

1. Is it legal?
2. Is it fair?
3. How do I feel about it?
4. Would the court of public opinion find my behavior incorrect?
5. Am I fearful of what those I trust would say about my actions?

Current Trends in Customer Service

1. Accessibility for the customer. Customers has more access to their accounts and thus has less questions to ask the customer service providers, In this cas the customer providers only have to ask more unique questions.
2. Immediacy of response. With the new technology being improved frequently, customer can have their questions answered and problems solved almost immediatly.
3. Feedback from customer to customer service provider. Customer suggestions are now frequently requested and encouraged. Customer suggestins logs are kept so that improvement can be made immediatly.
4. Out-sourcing of all or part of customer service functions.
5. Increasing use of technology to provide all levels of service.
